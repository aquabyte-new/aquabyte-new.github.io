Bryton Shang

Bryton Shang is the founder and CEO of Aquabyte, a Silicon Valley and Norway-based venture-backed company applying machine learning and computer vision to aquaculture fish farming for biomass estimation, sea lice counting, and feed optimization and formulation. Bryton was named to the 2019 Forbes 30 Under 30 in Manufacturing & Industry.

Graduating at the top of his engineering class at Princeton University, Bryton has led several venture-backed startups. Bryton built deep learning algorithms to diagnose cancer as CTO of HistoWiz, a biotechnology firm. He also co-founded iQ License, a brand licensing platform, and Nikao Investments, an algorithmic trading firm.

Alok Saxena

Ranked high in many US math competitions, Alok was also a member of the US Physics Team.

In his spare time, Alok enjoys social dancing.

Preben Imset Matre

Preben is educated as an aquaculture biologist at the NTNU, and has always been fascinated by the coupling of biology and technology. He has previously worked with sales at Lerøy, and for LEN, mapping the use and abuse of technology in the aquaculture sector.

At NTNU he was part of the founding of NTNU Ocean Club, an interdisciplinary student's group, focused on bridging the gap between students, researchers, and companies within the ocean space.

He enjoys games, downhill skiing, and hobby electronics.

Princeton Kwong

Princeton was formerly the Software Engineering Manager at Memebox Corporation, a $160 million venture-backed Y-Combinator company and a global e-commerce, recommendation, and community platform for Korean cosmetics with operations in the US, Korea, and greater China. He specializes in AI recommendation and facial recognition, distributed web systems, native mobile development, and data pipelines. Princeton has previously led software teams at venture-backed startups as a software architect, developer, and engineering manager.

He was also the Co-Founder and Software Lead at Rolli Digital, Inc., a mobile media-compression company. He holds his Master’s of Science in Computer Science from the University of Illinois and his Bachelor’s degree from Princeton University.

Princeton enjoys athletics and is a state ranked triathlete as well as a national champion figure skater.
